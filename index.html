My approach

I keep track of progress between lessons.

I try not to make tutoring feel like a one-off weekly hour. I keep track of what each student is finding difficult, what we’ve covered and what needs revisiting, so lessons build on each other properly.

A simple monitoring sheet for strengths, weaknesses, aims and targets
Tailored question packs and model answers where useful
Homework set and marked if requested
Progress discussions with parents where helpful

How lessons work

Structured online lessons without losing the personal feel.

Lessons are online using Microsoft Teams and an interactive whiteboard, but the setup is designed to make the work easy to follow and easy to revisit afterwards.

Work out the gaps

We identify weak topics, exam-board requirements and what the student wants to improve first.

Explain clearly

I break difficult ideas down using diagrams, examples and worked problems on the whiteboard.

Practise properly

We use selected past-paper questions and model answers, not random unfocused practice.

Keep revisiting

I keep track of what has improved and what still needs more work in future lessons.

Online classroom

A permanent whiteboard space for every student.

Each student gets their own online classroom. This means all the whiteboards from previous lessons stay saved in one place, instead of disappearing after the lesson ends.

  • Lessons delivered through Microsoft Teams and an online whiteboard
  • Past whiteboards remain available throughout the year
  • Whiteboards can be downloaded, saved or printed for revision
